On the "Tiny yet Real Happiness"
In the Mobile Games Market

by PoHeng Chen, YiPei Tu and Kuan-Ta Chen


Without tiny yet real happiness, I think life is like nothing but an insipid dessert.

Haruki Murakami in うずまき猫のみつけかた (1996)

This is a project I did in Data Insights Research Lab under the direction of Dr.SW Chen.
The paper is published in IEEE DSAA GDS 2016.

I try to understand why some Asian countries, such as Japan, Korea, Hong Kong, Singapore and Taiwan, spend disproportionately high on mobile gaming in a per-person base. I explain this circumstance by connecting to the Tiny yet Real Happiness phenomenon. The finding is that countries with long work hours, higher scores on the Gini index, lower unemployment rates, and lower life satisfaction are all associated with higher per-user spending on mobile games on both the App Store and Google Play platforms.
